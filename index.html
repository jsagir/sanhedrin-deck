End-to-End Flow

The Visitor Journey

01

Input Dilemma

Visitor arrives and inputs a personal moral dilemma via touchscreen interface.

02

Human Facilitator

The Game Master sets the stage. Human charisma is non-negotiable for live orchestration.

03

AI Orchestration

The "Nasi" profiles the visitor and selects 5–7 relevant thinkers by wisdom domain.

04

Deliberation

Selected thinkers and human participants deliberate, uncovering puzzle pieces together.

05

Synthesis

System highlights contradictions and expands perspective — no single "right answer".

06

Takeaway

Personalized digital summary with key insights and a reflection question via QR code.

Structured, repeatable 20–25 minute loop with human-in-the-loop orchestration

Role-Based
Architecture

This is not a standard chatbot array. We separate Persona from Role.

  • A persona can step into different functional roles depending on session needs
  • Humans and AI agents swap roles seamlessly

The Dilemma

The ancient "Goring Ox" (השור שנגח) modernized:

"Who is liable when an Autonomous Vehicle causes a fatal accident?"

Universal ethical gray zone (Tech/AI ethics) — no tribal political triggers

NASI assigns roles based on dilemma domain: Technology, Law, Ethics
✡ RAMBAM — The Judge
"Liability has layers. The programmer wrote the algorithm, but the manufacturer deployed it, and the city approved its roads. We must trace the chain of causation — each link bears its own weight of responsibility."
🕊 GOLDA MEIR — The Advocate
"Philosophy is beautiful, but families need answers now. The manufacturer must bear primary liability — they sold the promise of safety. Set the legal boundary first, refine later."
📖 HILLEL — The Questioner
"Before we assign blame — have we asked the pedestrian's family what justice means to them? Perhaps liability is less important than preventing the next accident."
🧩 3 PUZZLE PIECES UNCOVERED: Systemic Causation · Legal Pragmatism · Victim-Centered Justice
